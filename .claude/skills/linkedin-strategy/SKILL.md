# LinkedIn Strategy for Hardo App

Turn the brand into a repeatable publishing engine. Always apply `hardo-brand`.

## Content pillars (map every post to one)

Use a balanced mix so the feed isn't all-sell:

| Pillar | % of posts | Purpose | Example angle |
|--------|-----------|---------|----------------|
| **Teach** (technical/behavioral) | 35% | Authority, saves | "Walk me through a DCF — the clean version" |
| **Demystify the process** | 20% | Trust, reach | "What banks actually screen for in round 1" |
| **Confidence / mindset** | 15% | Relatability, comments | "Managing nerves when your mind goes blank" |
| **Proof / outcomes** | 15% | Conversion | candidate wins, before-after confidence |
| **Product / reps** | 15% | Signups | "I ran 20 mock interviews this week — here's what changed" |

Rule of thumb: **~80% value, ~20% direct product**. Even product posts should teach.

## Cadence

- **Starting cadence:** 3-4 posts/week (consistency > volume). Best windows for a
  student/finance audience: **Tue-Thu, ~8-10am or ~6-8pm** local to target market.
- **Format mix per week:** 2 text posts, 1 carousel, 1 story or video.
- Engage for 20-30 min right after posting (the first hour drives reach).
- Don't disappear on weekends entirely — 1 lighter post (mindset/relatable) does well.

## Editorial calendar — how to build one

When asked for a calendar, produce a table:

```
| Date | Pillar | Format | Working title / hook | CTA | Status |
```

- Default to a **2-week or 4-week** view.
- Balance pillars per the % targets above; never schedule two product posts back-to-back.
- Tie to the **recruiting calendar** — this is the highest-leverage timing lever:
  - **Aug-Oct:** summer-analyst applications open → peak anxiety → peak demand.
  - **Jan-Mar:** off-cycle / full-time / spring weeks.
  - **Exam seasons / term starts:** mindset + planning content.
- Seed 2-3 "evergreen" technical carousels you can re-run each cycle.

## Campaign planning

For a focused push (e.g. "Recruiting Season Sprint"), define:
1. **Objective + one metric** (e.g. +X profile-driven signups, or N saves).
2. **Theme & dates** (align to recruiting calendar).
3. **Content arc:** hook week (problem-aware) → value week (teach) → proof week
   (outcomes) → offer week (clear CTA / limited push).
4. **Hero asset:** one big carousel or mini-series as the centerpiece.
5. **Distribution:** founder profile + company page + asking the Insider/Multiplier
   personas (society leaders) to reshare.
6. **Measurement:** what you'll check at the end (see `linkedin-analytics`).

## Personal profile vs Company Page

- For an early-stage product, **the founder's personal profile usually out-reaches
  the company page 5-10x.** Lead with a personal profile; mirror key posts to the
  company page. Optimize the founder's headline + featured section to point to Hardo.

## Brand-voice review

When reviewing a draft, check against `hardo-brand`:
- ✅ Correct tone sliders (professional/sharp, empathetic, not hypey)?
- ✅ Technically accurate (no botched finance)?
- ✅ Maps to a pillar and a CTA (rotated, not repetitive)?
- ✅ Hook strong in first 2 lines? Link in first comment?
- ✅ No fabricated stats/quotes, no job guarantees?
Return a ✅/⚠️ checklist + a tightened rewrite.

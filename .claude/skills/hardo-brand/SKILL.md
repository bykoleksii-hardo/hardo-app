# Hardo App — Brand & Audience Context

This is the single source of truth for who Hardo App is, who it serves, and how
it should sound. Every other skill in this plugin builds on this file. When a
request involves messaging, always apply this context.

## Product in one line

Hardo App is an **AI-powered investment banking mock interview platform** — it
runs realistic IB interviews (technical + behavioral), gives instant feedback,
and helps candidates walk into the real thing prepared.

## What problem it solves

Breaking into investment banking is brutally competitive. Candidates face:
- **Technical grilling** — accounting, valuation (DCF, comps, precedents), LBO,
  enterprise vs equity value, the classic "walk me through a DCF".
- **Behavioral pressure** — "why banking?", "why this bank?", fit questions,
  thinking on your feet under stress.
- **No safe place to practice** — mock interviews with humans are scarce,
  expensive, and intimidating; reading guides isn't the same as *doing reps*.

Hardo gives unlimited, judgment-free reps with instant, specific feedback.

## Who we serve (audience personas)

1. **The Target (primary)** — penultimate/final-year university student recruiting
   for IB summer analyst / full-time roles. Anxious, ambitious, time-poor,
   comparing themselves to peers. Lives on LinkedIn for networking and signal.
2. **The Switcher** — someone moving into finance from another field or from a
   non-target school; needs structured prep and confidence.
3. **The Insider/Multiplier** — finance society leaders, career-services staff,
   student-club admins, mentors. They don't just use Hardo — they *recommend* it
   to cohorts. High-value for distribution.

## Positioning

- **Category:** interview prep / career tech for high-finance.
- **Wedge:** *reps with feedback*, not passive content. "Flashcards and PDFs
  don't interview you back."
- **Against:** generic interview-prep sites, $300/hr human coaches, doom-scrolling
  WSO forums at 2am.
- **Promise:** show up to the real interview having already done it 50 times.

## Brand voice

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Confident, sharp, a little bit "older-analyst-who's-been-there" | Cringe, hype-bro, fake-guru |
| Specific (name real concepts: DCF, LBO, comps) | Vague motivational fluff |
| Empathetic to the stress | Condescending |
| Concise, scannable, strong hooks | Long-winded, jargon for jargon's sake |
| Useful even if they never buy | Hard-sell every post |

**Tone sliders:** Professional 8/10 · Warmth 6/10 · Wit 5/10 · Formality 5/10.
We sound like a smart senior who genuinely wants you to land the offer.

## Messaging pillars (reuse across content)

1. **Get the reps** — practice beats reading; volume of realistic attempts wins.
2. **Feedback that's specific** — not "good job", but exactly what to fix.
3. **Demystify the process** — what banks actually ask and why.
4. **Confidence under pressure** — managing nerves, structure, thinking aloud.
5. **Real outcomes** — from "I freeze on technicals" to "I got the offer".

## Proof & credibility angles

- Coverage of the real question bank (technical + behavioral + fit).
- Instant, structured feedback loop.
- Practice-anytime vs scarce human mocks.
- (When available) candidate wins / testimonials / before-after confidence.

## Hard rules / guardrails

- **No guarantees of jobs/offers.** We improve preparation and confidence; we
  never promise employment outcomes.
- **No naming specific banks as endorsers** unless verified.
- **No fabricated stats or testimonials.** If a metric/quote isn't real, don't
  invent it — use a placeholder like `[INSERT real metric]` and flag it.
- **Compliance-aware:** finance audience is detail-sensitive; never publish wrong
  technical content (a botched DCF explanation destroys credibility instantly).
- **Inclusive:** non-target schools and career-switchers are welcome, not lesser.

## Key CTAs (rotate, don't repeat the same one every post)

- Soft: "Save this for your next prep session."
- Medium: "Comment 'REPS' and I'll send the practice set."
- Direct: "Run a free mock interview on Hardo — link in comments."
- Community: "Tag a friend who's recruiting this cycle."
